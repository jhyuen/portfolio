Figure Drawing

February 20, 2018

Description

At WPI, I participated in AR2200, also known as figure drawing. The professor assigned us to draw a figure from a magazine. Using a string connected to a fishing weight, I measured the dimensions of the figure. Then, I drew the figure and shaded in shadows based on classical art conventions. Because of my correct shading and attention to detail, I received an A for my work.

Providing Potable Water to Peruvian Informal Settlements

December 10, 2016

Description

WPI offers a variety of freshmen classes called Great Problems Seminars (GPS) that discuss humanity's great problems such as world hunger, poverty, the global water crisis, etc.

My GPS class explored the effects of the global water crisis, and my team was assigned to write a research paper about water purification in Peru. The goal of the project was to provide Manchay, a slum in the Pachacamac District of Lima, Peru, with a sustainable source of fresh and potable water. We researched technology that was independent of government-based water sources, and came to the conclusion that atmospheric water generators were the best solution. Because of our extensive research and comprehensive formal presentation, we achieved the highest score in the 2016 GPS Competition.

Best Leadership Practice by the Vietnamese Student Association (VSA)

December 14, 2017

Description

BUS1010 is a business leadership class offered at WPI designed to help students become future business leaders. The final project of the course is an analysis and recommendation summary on a WPI on-campus organization.

My team decided to research the WPI Vietnamese Student Association (VSA). We interviewed the VSA executives and compiled their responses. In our analysis and recommendation summary, we highlighted VSA's best leadership practices and then gave VSA recommendations on how it could improve. The professor held an in-class competition to determine which team performed the best analysis. My team won first place in the competition.

Brigham and Women's Hospital Navigation Pathfinding Application

March 2018 - May 2018

Description

CS3733 also known as Software Engineering is a class taught by Professor Wilson Wong at WPI. In the class, students are split up into teams of 10 and must make an application in 7 weeks.

When people visit loved ones at the hospital, many have a hard time figuring out where to go. As a result, my team's project was to make a pathfinding application for Brigham and Women's Hospital. The idea was that the software would be loaded on kiosks all around the hospital so that people could navigate their way through the hospital with minimal human assistance. The application's main feature is pathfinding in which a user can view the directions from an inputed start location to an inputed end location. Once the software has generated directions, a user can send them to their phone or email.

I was the team project manager, so I organized schedules and tasks for the rest of the team. Since I was also a software developer, I constructed the main map user interface.

Mafia Rising

March 2016 - July 2018

Description

In 2016, a couple of my friends and I formed a startup called Illum Softworks. One of our favorite childhood games was a party game called Mafia/Werewolf. As a result, in 2018, we released an iOS game based off of our favorite party game, and we called it Mafia Rising.

Mafia Rising is an innovative and immersive app for those who love role-playing party games that powerfully enhances the game atmosphere. My team and I created a random story generator with hundreds of interchangeable lines to produce thousands of stories. I worked with the programmers to build the game's progression system, and I also worked with the artists and musicians to create original content.
